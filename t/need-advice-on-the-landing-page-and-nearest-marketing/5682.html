Bobwalsh February 9, 2018, 4:13pm #2

Hi there,

  1. It’s clear what the service is, but not who the service is for.
  2. You need to explain what problem you propose to solve for what people.
  4. You don’t need a “killer” feature, but you need to offer a solution to a problem someone has. At first glance, this looks like it would be a good tool for companies that have a custom app using Mongo to open up the data stored in Mongo. Could be wrong on that - but you need to tell people what problem your product solves, and for who.
