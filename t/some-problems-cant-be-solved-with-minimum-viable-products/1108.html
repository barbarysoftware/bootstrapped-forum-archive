I think too many people make the mistake of thinking that an MVP is necessary for all products. It‚Äôs not, primarily because the purpose of an MVP is also misinterpreted. If you know something, then by definition, you don‚Äôt have to prove it and therefore do not need an MVP.

But if your question is ‚ÄúWill people pay for X‚Äù, where X is a product that does A, B and C, then you have to consider what your MVP is to test that hypothesis.
