Selling info-products it’a all about marketing, so you can’t blame something else for this. There are shitty ebooks that sell in millions because of good marketing and very good ebooks not selling at all.

Hi Chris,

Being one of those 4 people that purchased your book I’ll give you my 2 cents of what i’d try.

The book is written, it’s not going anywhere, keep it up and available

What i’d do with it…

Condense it down in to a lead generation tool, I’d include the what but only a little of the how and stick it on Amazon as a either a free ebook or a low cost ebook ($1.99 or less). It would of course contain lots of references to your website where they can find info on and buy one of the extended packages.

have one or two free downloads e.g. a checklist and a list of tools (with links to audacity downloads etc) that are available at the cost of signing up to the newsletter/giving you their email address (with verification of course) so you can start building your tribe.

Split the full book in to the condensed ‘what’ version and pull the ‘how’ bits out in to ‘courses’/‘tutorials’ that are included on purchase of a podcast ‘package’. Same/improved content just repackaged, books are seen as low cost cheap items, courses have a better perceived value.

host a forum somewhere to build a community and do a monthly/bi-weekly newsletter with a summary of answers from the forum or a tutorial / review of a tool useful for podcasting. Let the community generate content ideas etc for the newsletter.

If the sales go up, maybe start a blog and ‘new podcasts’ review

hopefully there are some ideas there.

good luck with whichever way you go.
